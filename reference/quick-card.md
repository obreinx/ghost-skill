# Ghost Quick Reference Card

## 30-Second Human Pass
1. Number your sentences by word count. Find same-length runs. Break them.
2. Replace 3 predictable words with less obvious synonyms.
3. Insert one "I think" / "honestly" / "look" every 150 words.
4. Delete your intro sentence, end on an example not a conclusion.
5. Read aloud. Does it sound like a person? Done.

## Cheat Sheet

### Sentence Structure (burstiness)
```
Fragments (<6 words):   min 3 per 500 words
Run-ons (30+ words):    min 2 per 500 words
"And" or "But" starts:  min 2 per 500 words
Single-sentence grafs:  min 2 per 1000 words
```

### Word Choice (perplexity)
```
Obvious word replacements: 12-15% of total words
Colloquial alternatives:   1-2 per paragraph
Downgrade formality:       prefer "use" over "utilize"
```

### Human Markers (voice)
```
First person:             at least 1 per 500 words
Opinion / stance:         at least 1 per 300 words
Aside / digression:       at least 1 per 500 words
Colloquial connector:     at least 1 per 250 words
```

### Forbidden AI Tells
```
NEVER: delve, unleash, realm, tapestry, landscape,
       furthermore, moreover, nevertheless, consequently,
       in conclusion, it is worth noting, not only... but also,
       a testament to, underscores, in today's world
```

## Detector Quick-Reference

| Detector | Main Signal | Weakest Against |
|---|---|---|
| GPTZero | Perplexity + Burstiness | Dialogue, fragments, first-person |
| Originality | Perplexity (heavy) | Unpredictable word choice, colloquialisms |
| Turnitin | Academic structure | Broken templates, no thesis-conclusion |
| ZeroGPT | Perplexity only | Any burstiness at all |
| CopyLeaks | Repetition patterns | Varied paragraph structure |
| Winston | GPT-output fingerprints | Subjective opinions, emotion |

## Emergency Bypass (15 seconds)
Break one long sentence into fragments. Combine two short sentences. Done.
